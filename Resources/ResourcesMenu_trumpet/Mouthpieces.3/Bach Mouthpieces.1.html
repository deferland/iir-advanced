Bach Mouthpieces

Vincent Bach combined his unique talents as both a musician and an engineer to create brass instruments which remain the sound choice of artists worldwide. As a trumpet player on tour in Pittsburgh, a repairman convinced Vincent that he could improve his mouthpiece. After ruining the mouthpiece, Vincent experienced difficulty in finding a suitable replacement. He then recognized the need by brass players for high-quality mouthpieces. He began making and selling his line of mouthpieces in 1918.

Bach mouthpieces are the most popular mouthpieces in the world used by beginners and professionals alike. Like trumpets, its always a good idea to try out various mouthpieces for extended playing sessions before making any final decisions on what might be the best mouthpiece for you. Below are some of the most common Bach mouthpieces used by trumpet players.

1 C

Medium cup, medium wide rim; for powerful players, compact tone of great carrying power for a symphonic sound

1 C

Medium cup, medium wide rim (not too sharp); good mouthpiece for all around playing

3 C

Medium cup, medium wide rim; good all around use, for players able to use a large mouthpiece who want an easier high register and brilliant tone

5 C

Medium cup, medium wide rim (well rounded toward the inside and outside fairly flat); for players with a strong embouchure who do not like a sharp edge, lively and rich tone

7 C

Medium cup, medium wide rim (lowered toward the outside, medium sharp inside with well rounded edge); the most used model in the world and known for its brilliant tone, a very popular mouthpiece for beginners who are still developing their embouchures

5 B

Medium deep cup, medium wide rim (lowered toward the outside with a medium sharp edge); a precise rim  and a fairly large cup, tone is vivid an full

1 B

Medium deep cup, medium wide rim; large cup for players with a robust embouchure, produces a warm tone with great volume
