Schilke Mouthpieces

Renold Otto Schilke, a former trumpet player with the Chicago Symphony orchestra, started the Schilke company in 1956. As a boy, he was a member of the Holton factory band and grew up learning the art of music instrument manufacturing. By age 17, Schilke was making his own mouthpieces.

Although not as widely used as Bach, Schilke mouthpieces are very popular with many professional players and are known for their high quality and excellent design.

11

A superior mouthpiece with standard characteristics for a free blowing mouthpiece; closest to the Bach 7C and popular for young players

13

Ideal choice for a student or developing player with standard #3 rim contour. Rounder rim provides increased flexibility and quick response. "C" cup offers volume for tone; more cushioned rim, close to a Bach 5C - more advanced

13C4

A somewhat sharp inner-edge combined with a funnel-shaped "C" cup offers a clear tone. The #4 semi-flat rim adds increased endurance

14

With a standard rim, cup and backbore, the 14 is an excellent choice for all around playing; a Bach 1 C player might like this one

16C4

Similar to the 16 in dimensions, this mouthpiece offers a semi-flat rim for a more traditional feel and increased endurance; most similar feel to a Bach 1 C

17

A compromise of all the medium characteristics, this mouthpiece is an excellent medium to large diameter mouthpiece; similar to a Bach 1 C

18

The high point being nearer to the center makes this mouthpiece feel smaller than it is. The standard characteristics produce a rich, brilliant tone; similar to a Bach 1 C

12B4

This medium-small conical "B" cup provides a full tone and the semi-flat rim allows for more endurance; this bigger diameter and shallower cup is close to a Bach 6D
