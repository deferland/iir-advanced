Straight Mutes

The straight mute is the most commonly used mute. Straight mutes are designed with a variety of different materials, but the most common are aluminum, brass, or copper. For a softer sound, mutes can be made out of plastic, cardboard, or wood. Every material influences the sound of the mute differently, so try different mutes to see which one fits the sound you are looking for. In the orchestra, we generally play aluminum straight mute for ordinary playing, a copper-bottom straight mute for loud playing, and a cardboard or wood mute for the softer, more delicate playing.
