Mouthpiece Info

A standard mouthpiece for a beginning trumpeter is a Bach 7c. However, you may want to try a different sized mouthpiece, as there are countless varieties. When looking for a new mouthpiece, keep in mind that every player has different sized lips and strength abilities. In general, a bigger mouthpiece will warm and darken the sound. For Bach mouthpieces, the lower numbers mean a larger rim diameter. A bigger mouthpiece might better suit a more advanced player, as it takes more strength and stamina to play a larger rim. A smaller mouthpiece might help a student play higher, but possibly at the cost of sacrificing tone quality and warmth of sound. It is ideal to find a balance between ease of playing and sound. The letter after the number refers to the shape of the cup. A "C" cup will have a different depth and feel to it then a "B" cup (which has a more funnel shape).
