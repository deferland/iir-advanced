Bb Professional Trumpets

The most important thing about picking a professional trumpet is to take your time and pick the one that feels best for you. Make sure and try out many professional horns before making a final selection. For the amount of money you are going to spend on a professional horn, it's important to make the right choice. I also recommend playing each horn for a couple days if there is any way possible, rather than just playing a few notes before making your final decision.

Below are some of the most popular professional trumpets available today. Although this isn't a complete list, the represents a good selection of trumpets perfect for advanced high school students, college players and professionals alike. They represent trumpets that are good for most common playing situations from concert band to orchestra to big band work. Most of these trumpets can be customized with various bell sizes, lead pipes, finishes and other options.

Bach Stradivarius 180

The most popular professional trumpet today, perfect for all around playing; .459" Medium-large bore, standard weight yellow brass, #37 bell, monel pistons, clean lacquer finish

Bach Stradivarius LR180

This Stradivarius comes in a lightweight body with a reverse construction #25LR mouthpipe as well as having a clean lacquer finish

Bach Stradivarius LT180

In addition to having a lightweight body, this trumpet is constructed using lightweight yellow brass

Bach Stradivarius 182

Very similar to the 180 model, however this trumpet comes in a lightweight body and a silver-plate finish,

Yamaha YTR-6335

This popular trumpet has a bore size of .459" ML; standard monel pistons, pressure-formed tubing for smoother air flow, Laser-fused pluzuma welded bell and a single brace on the tuning slide for slightly darker sound

Yamaha YTR-6345

Very similar to the YTR-6335, but this trumpet has a larger bore size of .462", perfect for a large rich sound

Jupiter XO 1602

One of the newest professional trumpets on the market; ML bore of .459", silver-plated yellow brass, monel pistons, elliptical and rounded tuning slides, one piece hand hammered 4.8" bell and two piece valve casing

Jupiter XO 1604

Very similar to the Jupiter XO 1602, this trumpet has a larger bore size of .462 for a large rich sound
