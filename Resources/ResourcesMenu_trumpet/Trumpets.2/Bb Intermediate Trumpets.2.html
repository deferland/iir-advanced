Bb Intermediate Trumpets

After a few years of playing, students often find that moving to a "step up" trumpet can be of benefit, especially as they enter high school. Improved intonation and sound quality are important in the next steps of development for trumpet players. Picking a good intermediate trumpet can assist in those areas.

Below are some popular "step up" trumpets on the market today. It is often a good idea to consult with your band director or private instructor as to which instruments they feel would be best for you before making a purchase. Some of these instruments are available in either a gold or silver finish.

Bach TR200

Plays with a centered sound and good response, perfect for the advancing player; .459" bore, first valve slide, adjustable third valve slide, lacquered finish, monel metal pistons and enclosed valve spring

Yamaha YTR-4335G

Many professional features make this trumpet ideal; monel pistons, pressure-formed tubing, laser-fused pluzuma welded bell, hand-lapped pistons and slides

Jupiter 1002S

One of the newer intermediate trumpets on the market today; .460" bore, 1st valve slide thumb saddle, 3rd valve throw ring and stop, flared 5" bell, medium weight, monel pistons, silver-plated yellow brass

Blessing XL-TR

Designed for the advancing student; .460" bore, 1st valve slide trigger and adjustable 3rd valve fingering ring, monel valves, 5" diameter bell, lacquer finish

Holton ST550 Admiral Series

Responsive, easy blowing characteristics; .459" bore, 1st slide U-hook, adjustable 3rd valve slide stop-nut, monel valves, 4.8" plasma welded bell, clean baked epoxy lacquer finish

Getzen 700 Series Eterna

Professional quality at an intermediate price; .460" bore size, 4.75" two piece yellow brass bell, first side saddle and adjustable third slide ring, hand lapped gold brass slides, bight silver plate finish
