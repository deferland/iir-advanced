JAZZ MOUTHPIECES

There are many models of jazz mouthpieces on the market today, which help saxophonists in the quest for their personal sound. And as when choosing a classical mouthpiece, selecting a good jazz mouthpiece is critical; many players would agree that the mouthpiece and reed combination is actually the most important part of the saxophone, as it has the largest influence over our tone, intonation, articulation, and response.

Jazz saxophone mouthpieces are most often made of hard rubber or metal, while a few specialty models are made of wood. Keep in mind however that the tone is more influenced by the cut of the mouthpiece - the tip opening, the length of the lay or facing, and the baffle and chamber - than by the material itself. I recommend that young saxophonists begin with medium-opening hard rubber mouthpieces (for instance, the hard rubber Meyer 5M is a great place to start for alto players), which will serve them well in many situations.

The list below should be considered a introduction to mouthpieces that are good for jazz and pop music, whether in a jazz ensemble, combo, or band. I have also included some notes about sizes to consider, or if I recommend the mouthpiece for a particular saxophone. Different mouthpiece manufacturers have differing measurement systems, so it is helpful to refer to a mouthpiece comparison chart to find mouthpieces of similar sizes. And as with all mouthpieces, it is important to try a few when making a purchase. Even identical mouthpieces of the same make and size can still be quite different from one another.

Bari

Opening: 58-62. Soprano mouthpiece recommended.

Beechler (Diamond)

Hard rubber or metal; 5-6 opening, S or M chamber. Alto mouthpiece recommended.

Berg Larsen

Hard rubber or metal. Alto, tenor, baritone mouthpieces recommended.

Claude Lakey

Hard rubber, 4*3 or 4*4. Alto mouthpiece recommended.

Dukoff

Metal. These tend to be brighter mouthpieces with a bit more 'bite'.

Francois Louis

Metal, hard rubber, and wood. Very fine workmanship, and also fairly expensive.

Guardala

Models: King, Crescent, Super King, MB II

Jody Jazz

Hard rubber or metal.

Lawton

Metal. Recommend 5*-8.

Meyer

Hard rubber; 5M or 6M. Alto mouthpiece recommended.

Morgan Jazz

Opening: 5-8. Chamber: M, ML, L.

Otto Link

Hard rubber or metal; 6-8 tip opening.

Ponzol

Models: Stainless Steel, M2, Vintage, M2 Plus, Hard Rubber Vintage, Ponzol Vintage.

Selmer (vintage "Soloist" or newer Super Session)

Hard rubber; D - F facing.

Vandoren

Models: V5 Jazz, Java, Jumbo Java, V16.

Yanagisawa

Metal: 6-8. Hard Rubber: 5-8.
