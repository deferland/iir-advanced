Student Saxophones

There are a number of makes and models of student instruments on the market today. These instruments are created as an entry point into playing the saxophone, and traditionally have nickel-plated rods and keywork. The craftsmanship, intonation, tone, and evenness are not what you would find in an intermediate or professional level instrument, but these instruments can be adequate for a beginning saxophonist.

The equipment a beginning student starts with is very important, and a substandard or poorly regulated instrument can hinder a student's development and enthusiasm for music. For that reason, even when buying a first instrument, it is important to choose wisely, preferably with the help of a teacher or experienced saxophonist.

I recommend not spending a lot of money on a student level instrument; try to buy a good quality used instrument, and have it checked by a technician to ensure that it is in good condition and properly regulated. Another alternative is to rent until you know you'd like to continue. When possible, try to start with an intermediate level instrument, which you won't outgrow as quickly.

Below is a listing of some student level saxophones for consideration. In particular, I recommend either the Yamaha YAS-23 or Selmer USA AS500 at the student level.

  • Yamaha Standard Alto Saxophone YAS/YTS-23
  • Selmer (USA) AS500
  • Selmer Prelude AS700
  • Keilwerth ST90 IV
