Overview

There are dozens of saxophone manufacturers producing instruments today, and when shopping for a saxophone it can be a bit daunting to know where to begin, particularly for the first-time buyer. I have included some recommendations when looking for a student, intermediate, or professional level saxophone. There are a few important things to keep in mind, for any saxophonist. First, treat your instrument with great care, and have it serviced by a professional repairperson regularly. All saxophones go out of adjustment, leading to pad leaks and other issues which can make even professional instruments difficult to play. Second, while the choice of instrument is very important, the choice of a quality mouthpiece and reed combination will go equally far (if not farther) in determining your tone. And third, a great instrument will only be as good as the saxophonist playing it, so be sure to find a good teacher in your area from whom to take regular lessons.
