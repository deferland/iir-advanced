Intermediate Saxophones

Intermediate level instruments feature better workmanship than student line saxophones, and the components are generally of a higher quality. These saxophones also often include a better mouthpiece, a high F# key, brass keys, and more elaborate engraving. Their tone and intonation will be superior to student instruments as well. When purchasing an intermediate level saxophone, keep in mind that the price for a new intermediate instrument is often not far off from that of a used professional saxophone, so keep an eye out for used horns that are in good condition. My personal recommendations in this category are the Selmer La Voix or Yamaha YAS-475, or a used Yamaha 52.

  • Selmer La Voix II
  • Yamaha YAS/YTS-475
  • Yamaha 52 (no longer in production, but available on the used market)
  • Keilwerth EX90
  • Yanagisawa 901
  • Cannonball Sceptyr
