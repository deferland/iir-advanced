Brio Flutes

Brio flutes come from the Gemeinherdt flute company and they produce a series of intermediate flutes. The flute has a sturdy frame and a pretty smooth action, but the highlight is the headjoint, which is handmade by Dana Sheridan. Dana Sheridan makes some the highest quality professional headjoints, and for Brio to have this partnership really elevates the level of this flute from an average, to a good intermediate flute. The cut of the Dana Sheridan headjoint produces a brilliant sound, which is more substantial than Azumi, but still brighter than Avanti. Another positive feature is the professional look of the instrument, with its French (pointed key) arms and gold springs.

Brio B10-OSB/SB (offset/inline G)

French or open hole, sterling silver handcut Dana Sheridan series headjoint, silver plated body, footjoint and keys, silver plated mechanism, offset/ inline G, stainless steel springs, dapped (Y-arm) keywork, B footjoint

Brio B1-OSB/SB (offset/inline G)

Same as B10 but with gold springs. Gold springs may marginally add to the sound.

Brio B20-OSB/SB (offset/inline G)

French or open hole, sterling silver handcut Dana Sheridan series headjoint, silver body and footjoint, silver plated keys, offset/inline G, stainless steel springs, dapped (Y-arm) keywork, B footjoint.

Brio B2-OSB/SB

Same as B20 except with gold springs.

Brio B2.4-OSB/SB

Flute is 9K rose gold plated. Gold plated flutes add a slight amount of the warmth of the solid gold flutes, but at a much more reasonable price. Gold plated is not a substitute for a gold flute!

Brio B3-OSSB/SSB

French or open hole, sterling silver handcut Dana Sheridan series headjoint, sterling silver body, footjoint and keys, offset /inline G, gold springs, pointed key arms, B footjoint
